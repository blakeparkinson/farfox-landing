An anniversary apart hits differently. It's the one day the distance feels most pointed — a celebration of *us* spent in two different places. But with a little coordination, a long-distance anniversary can be just as meaningful as one spent together. Sometimes more, because it takes more intention.

Here are 30 ideas, organized by how you want the day to feel.

---

## Celebrate in real time (virtual date)

1. **Recreate your first date** over video — same meal, same dressed-up energy.
2. **Synchronized dinner** — order the same cuisine, set the table, eat "together."
3. **Movie premiere night** — watch the film from around when you met (Teleparty keeps you synced).
4. **Read your letters aloud.** Each write one beforehand; read them to each other at the date's end.
5. **Virtual toast at the same minute** — coordinate across time zones and clink to the camera.
6. **Slideshow date** — build a shared photo recap of your year and watch it together.
7. **Take an online class together** — a cooking or cocktail class makes a memorable anniversary activity.

For more on pulling off a great virtual date, see our [long-distance date ideas](/blog/long-distance-date-ideas) guide.

## Surprise them from afar

8. **Send dinner to their door** mid-date so you're literally eating the same meal.
9. **Flowers or a surprise delivery** timed to arrive that morning.
10. **A scavenger hunt** you coordinate with a friend or via hidden notes from your last visit.
11. **Hijack their day** — arrange a coffee delivery, a playlist that drops at noon, a letter in their mailbox.
12. **Surprise visit** — if it's at all possible, nothing beats showing up. (If not, plan one and reveal the date.)

## Give something lasting

13. **A photo book of your year** — auto-built from your shared photos.
14. **A love-letter book** — your letters from the year, printed and bound.
15. **A time capsule** — write messages to open at your next reunion, or next anniversary.
16. **Matching items** — something you each wear or use that connects you.
17. **A piece of jewelry or a watch** set to their time zone.
18. **A star map** of the night you met, or your first date.

Need more gift inspiration? Our [long-distance relationship gifts](/blog/long-distance-relationship-gifts) guide has ideas that travel well.

## Make it a tradition

19. **Annual question list** — answer the same set of questions every anniversary and watch your answers change.
20. **The countdown reveal** — announce the date of your next visit as the "gift."
21. **A yearly letter** — one long letter each, every anniversary, kept forever.
22. **Re-watch your "wedding" — er, your first video call.** If you have it. Cringe together.
23. **Plant something** in each of your homes on the same day, every year.

## When you're on a budget

24. **Handwritten letter + a single meaningful photo.** Free, and it lands harder than anything bought.
25. **A homemade video montage** of the year.
26. **Cook their favorite meal "together"** over call.
27. **A playlist that tells your story** — one song per chapter.
28. **A 365-reasons jar**, photographed and sent (or mailed).
29. **A long voice note** saying everything you'd say in person.
30. **Plan the future out loud** — the most romantic free thing two people apart can do.

## The thing that makes any anniversary land

Whatever you choose, two moves elevate it from "nice phone call" to "I'll remember this":

- **Coordinate the timing** so it genuinely feels shared, in real time.
- **Add something they can keep** — a letter, a photo book, a recording — so the day outlasts the call.
