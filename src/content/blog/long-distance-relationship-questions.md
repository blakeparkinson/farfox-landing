When you live apart, conversations carry more weight. You can't rely on shared dinners or lazy Sunday mornings to fill in the gaps — the words you exchange *are* the relationship, at least until you're together again.

The problem? Most long-distance couples get stuck in the same loop: "how was your day," "what did you eat," "miss you," "goodnight." It's comfortable. It's also how you stop *really* knowing each other.

Here are 100 questions to break that loop — grouped by mood, from light and silly to deep and vulnerable. Use them on a call, in a text, or take turns answering one every day.

## Deep questions to ask your long-distance partner

These are the ones that make you pause before answering.

1. What's something you've changed your mind about in the last year?
2. When do you feel most like yourself?
3. What's a fear you've never told me about?
4. What did your parents get right about love? What did they get wrong?
5. What's a version of yourself you'd like to grow into?
6. What do you think is the hardest part of being with me?
7. When was the last time you cried, and what was it about?
8. What's a compliment that stuck with you for years?
9. What does "home" mean to you — is it a place, a person, or a feeling?
10. What's something you're proud of that no one knows about?
11. What would a perfectly ordinary day with me look like?
12. What do you wish I asked you more often?
13. What's a memory of us you replay when we're apart?
14. What's your biggest insecurity right now?
15. What's something you're grateful for about our relationship today?

## Fun and playful questions

Not every conversation needs to go deep. These keep things light and remind you that you actually *like* each other.

16. If our relationship had a theme song, what would it be?
17. What's the most ridiculous thing you'd do if you won the lottery tomorrow?
18. If we were both cartoon characters, who would we be?
19. What's your most controversial food opinion?
20. What's a skill you wish you had, even if it's useless?
21. What's the weirdest thing you find attractive about me?
22. If we could have dinner with any three people, who would we pick?
23. What's your go-to karaoke song?
24. What fictional world would you want to live in for a week?
25. What's your most embarrassing childhood memory?
26. What's a small thing that always makes you laugh?
27. If we were stranded on a desert island, what three things would you want?
28. What's your unpopular opinion about a popular movie?
29. What animal would I be, and what animal would you be?
30. What's the most unhinged thing you've ever Googled?

## Relationship and future questions

For when you want to talk about where you're headed — not just where you are.

31. Where do you see us in five years?
32. What does our ideal shared home look like?
33. What city have we never been to that you'd love to live in together?
34. What's something you want us to do together before we're 40?
35. What's a tradition you want us to start?
36. What's a dealbreaker you didn't know you had until recently?
37. How do you want us to handle disagreements better?
38. What's something you'd like us to stop doing?
39. What's something you'd like us to start doing?
40. When you picture our wedding (or next big milestone), what feels most important?
41. What do you want our mornings to look like when we live together?
42. What's a lesson you've learned from a past relationship?
43. What's something I do that makes you feel loved without me realizing?
44. How do you want us to celebrate our next anniversary?
45. What do you want our lives to look like in 10 years?

46. What's the first thing you want to do when we're back together?
47. What's a moment with me you think about when you can't sleep?
48. What do you miss most about me physically?
49. What was your first impression of me — honestly?
50. What's something I wear that you can't stop thinking about?
51. What's the most romantic thing anyone's ever done for you?
52. What's the most romantic thing you want to do for me?
53. When did you know you were falling for me?
54. What's a compliment you've been meaning to give me?
55. If we had 24 hours together right now, what would you want to do?
56. What's your favorite photo of us, and why?
57. What's a small thing I do that you find attractive?
58. What's a memory with me you wish you could relive?
59. What's something you want to try with me next time we're together?
60. What does your perfect kiss look like?

## Would You Rather questions for couples

76. Would you rather live by the ocean or in the mountains together?
77. Would you rather travel for a year or buy a house?
78. Would you rather have a huge wedding or elope?

81. Would you rather never argue or always make up quickly?

84. Would you rather lose all our texts or all our photos?
85. Would you rather have a tiny apartment in a city you love or a huge house in the middle of nowhere?

97. What's been making you feel closer to me, even when we're apart?
98. What's a promise you want us to make to each other this month?
99. What's one thing you'd want me to know if we never spoke again?
100. What's something you want to thank me for that you haven't said yet?

## How to actually use these questions

A list is only as good as the rhythm you build around it. A few suggestions:

- **Don't blitz them.** Asking 20 questions in one call feels like an interview. Pick one a day.
- **Take turns.** One of you picks the question, the other answers first. Swap the next day.
- **Write your answers.** Typed or handwritten answers let you both be more thoughtful than you'd be on a call.
- **Wait for both answers.** Don't let your partner's answer shape yours. Answer independently, then compare.

## The questions aren't the point

The questions are just a door. What matters is the habit of walking through it — the ritual of asking something real, every day, even when you're tired, even when nothing exciting happened, even when you're 3,000 miles apart.

Couples who last long-distance aren't the ones with the most dramatic FaceTime dates. They're the ones who kept asking each other real questions long after the honeymoon phase ended.
